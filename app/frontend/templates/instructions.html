Texas Hold'em

Basic Rules

  • Each player is dealt two private cards (known as "hole cards").
  • Five community cards are dealt face-up on the "board".
  • Players use these seven cards to make the best five-card hand.
  • The game consists of four betting rounds: Pre-flop, Flop, Turn, and River.
  • Players can bet, check, call, raise, or fold during their turn.

Hand Rankings

  • Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  • Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  • Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: Five consecutive cards of different suits.
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair: Two different pairs.
  • One Pair: One pair of the same rank.
  • High Card: The highest card if no other hand is made.

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-flop: After receiving their hole cards, players bet.
  • Flop: The first three community cards are dealt face-up, followed by a betting round.
  • Turn: The fourth community card is dealt face-up, followed by another betting round.
  • River: The fifth and final community card is dealt face-up, followed by the final betting round.

Omaha Poker

Basic Rules

  • Each player is dealt four private cards.
  • Five community cards are dealt face-up on the "board".
  • Players must use exactly two of their private cards and three of the community cards to make the best five-card hand.
  • The game consists of four betting rounds: Pre-flop, Flop, Turn, and River.
  • Players can bet, check, call, raise, or fold during their turn.

Hand Rankings

  • Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  • Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  • Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: Five consecutive cards of different suits.
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair: Two different pairs.
  • One Pair: One pair of the same rank.
  • High Card: The highest card if no other hand is made.

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-flop: After receiving their private cards, players bet.
  • Flop: The first three community cards are dealt face-up, followed by a betting round.
  • Turn: The fourth community card is dealt face-up, followed by another betting round.
  • River: The fifth and final community card is dealt face-up, followed by the final betting round.

Seven-Card Stud

Basic Rules

  • Each player is dealt seven cards, three down and four up, over the course of five rounds.
  • The goal is to make the best five-card hand from the seven cards received.
  • The game consists of five betting rounds.
  • Players can bet, check, call, raise, or fold during their turn.

Hand Rankings

  • Royal Flush: A, K, Q, J, 10, all of the same suit.
  • Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit.
  • Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank.
  • Full House: Three of a kind and a pair.
  • Flush: Five cards of the same suit.
  • Straight: Five consecutive cards of different suits.
  • Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank.
  • Two Pair: Two different pairs.
  • One Pair: One pair of the same rank.
  • High Card: The highest card if no other hand is made.

Betting Rounds

  • Third Street: Each player is dealt three cards, two down and one up. Betting starts with the player showing the lowest card.
  • Fourth Street: Each player is dealt a fourth card face-up. Betting starts with the player showing the highest hand.
  • Fifth Street: Each player is dealt a fifth card face-up. Betting starts with the player showing the highest hand.
  • Sixth Street: Each player is dealt a sixth card face-up. Betting starts with the player showing the highest hand.
  • Seventh Street: Each player is dealt a seventh and final card face-down. Betting starts with the player showing the highest hand.
